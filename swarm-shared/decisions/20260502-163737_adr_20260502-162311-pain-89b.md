## v0.1 scope
v0.1 ships in 2 weeks: Android client (minSdk 26) with hidden SOCKS5 proxy on ephemeral ports, userspace WireGuard (boringtun) with TUN interface obfuscation, two DPI-evasion profiles (mimic-HTTPS and meek), local encrypted SQLite policy/cache, Supabase-hosted config + health telemetry, and Cloudflare Workers + KV for global egress allow-lists; no account/PII required, works on stock Android without MDM/Knox.

## Open questions
- Which TUN concealment techniques remain effective on Android 12+ without root or custom kernels?
- How to reliably hide ephemeral SOCKS5 ports from /proc/net and ss/netstat inspection on stock Android?
- What cover-traffic profiles minimize latency while defeating DPI (mimicry vs random padding vs meek)?
- Can we avoid declaring a VPN service in package-visible listings while still routing traffic through a TUN?
- Which egress fronting strategies (domain-front, meek, snowflake) are reachable from restrictive jurisdictions today?
- How to measure WAUS/U locally without leaking telemetry that deanonymizes users?
