## Deployment
cf workers

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 in 2 weeks: local-only stealth client (Rust CLI/WASM) that hides SOCKS5 listening ports by remapping to allowed cover ports (443/53), presents innocuous TLS/HTTP cover traffic to local DPI probes, and enforces a fail-closed killswitch that blocks all non-stealth traffic if evasion fails; local SQLite state + minimal CF Workers edge endpoint for signed config/feature flags; no external proxy or TUN required in v0.1; client-side telemetry hashed/pseudonymized to Supabase for WAU-ZB measurement.

## Open questions
- Which DPI signatures (SNI, JA3, packet timing, TTL anomalies) are most reliable indicators in target regions and can we mitigate them client-side without servers?
- Can we implement robust TUN concealment on desktop OSes without kernel drivers while preserving throughput?
- What is the acceptable latency budget for obfuscation (meek/tls-framing) before usability degrades?
- How will we validate WAU-ZB with privacy-preserving telemetry that avoids deanonymization?
- Which cover traffic patterns (DNS/HTTP/TLS) appear most innocuous to local DPI and minimize collateral blocking?
