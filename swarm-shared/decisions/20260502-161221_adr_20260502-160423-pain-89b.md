## Deployment
cf workers

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 ships a CLI stealth client (Rust) that: creates a local SOCKS5 listener, tunnels traffic through a Shadowsocks-obfs or meek fronted path via CF Workers, masks local port/TUN artifacts from localhost probes, enforces a fail-closed kill-switch on stealth path failure, and emits encrypted WAU-ZB telemetry to Supabase Postgres — all client-side, zero-config, deployable in 2 weeks.

## Open questions
- Which obfuscation primitives (meek, shadowsocks-obfs, v2ray-xtls) maximize reach under Russian DPI while remaining implementable client-side only?
- How will clients discover/rotate fronting endpoints without centralized infra (CF Workers + DNS TXT vs baked-in lists)?
- What kill-switch granularity is required (per-app vs system-wide) to prevent leaks on desktop clients?
- How to collect WAU-ZB telemetry without deanonymizing users or creating a targeting surface?
- Can TUN interface creation be hidden or delayed on Windows/macOS to evade port scans and service enumeration?
