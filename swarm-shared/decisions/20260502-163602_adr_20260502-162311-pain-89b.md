- `src/core/net/ (TUN/SOCKS5 obfuscation, packet mangling, traffic morphing)`

- `src/core/obfs/ (port-hiding, interface cloaking, traffic shaping)`
- `src/platform/android/ (VPNService-less TUN via tun2socks/fd splicing, JNI bindings)`
- `src/control/ (enrollment, config, heartbeat, CF Workers edge endpoints)`
- `src/infra/db/ (Supabase clients, local SQLite migrations)`
- `src/infra/obs/ (minimal structured logs, counters for WAUS/U)`
- `tests/unit/ (crypto, obfs, net primitives)`
- `tests/integration/ (end-to-end reachability under mock DPI)`

## Deployment
cf workers

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 in 2 weeks: Android client (minSdk 24) with tun2socks-based TUN->SOCKS5 forwarding, Noise-based session handshake, two traffic-morphing profiles (HTTP/1 stream and QUIC-like), Cloudflare Workers control plane for config/certs (KV), Supabase Postgres for user/session metadata, local SQLite for ephemeral state, and basic WAUS/U counters; supports manual endpoint enrollment and one-tap connect/disconnect with no root or VPNService required.

## Open questions
- Which traffic morphing profiles are safe against common DPI in target jurisdictions (e.g., HTTP/1 streaming vs QUIC-like vs TLS-in-TLS)?
- Can we reliably hide TUN interfaces on stock Android without root or VPNService while still forwarding traffic (tun2socks/fd approach) and remain performant?
- How will we distribute and rotate fronting endpoints/certs without centralized infra that can be blocked?
- What is the acceptable false-positive rate for obfuscation (degraded throughput/latency) vs detectability?
- How do we measure WAUS/U locally without leaking telemetry that identifies users?
- Which port-knocking/rendezvous scheme balances usability and deniability for initial bootstrap?
