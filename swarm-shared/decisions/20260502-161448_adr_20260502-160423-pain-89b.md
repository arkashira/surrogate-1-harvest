## Deployment
cf workers

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 ships a single-client stealth binary (Linux/macOS/Windows) that creates a local SOCKS5 listener, forwards traffic through a userspace TUN/WireGuard-like tunnel via a meek/TLS-obfuscated bridge (hosted on CF Workers + free-tier origin), enforces a fail-closed kill-switch, and emits minimal encrypted client-side telemetry for WAU-ZB; no server management required and all deps OSS.

## Open questions
- Which DPI-evasion primitives are safest and most effective in target regions (TLS-fragmentation vs. meek vs. shadowsocks-style stream obfuscation)?
- How will the client discover/rotate bridge endpoints without leaking metadata (bootstrap via CF Workers + DoH vs. pre-shared list)?
- What telemetry minimization and encryption-at-rest choices balance WAU-ZB measurement with deniability/privacy?
- How to implement a reliable cross-platform kill-switch (network namespace rules, firewall ACLs, or split-tunnel routing) without admin/root on all OSes?
- How to test DPI resistance locally without controlled probes that resemble real blocking infrastructure?
