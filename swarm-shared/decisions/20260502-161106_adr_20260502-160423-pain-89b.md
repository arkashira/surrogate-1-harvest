## Deployment

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 ships a cross-platform CLI stealth client (Rust) that: creates a hidden local SOCKS5 listener (port randomization + socket cloaking), establishes a DTLS 1.3-disguised tunnel to a free-tier CF Worker edge endpoint (acting as a minimal bridge to egress), enforces a fail-closed kill-switch (block non-tunnel traffic while active), and emits encrypted, minimal session events to Supabase Postgres for WAU-ZB measurement — all client-side, zero-config, no admin required.

## Open questions
- Which disguise transport (DTLS 1.3 mimicry vs. ShadowTLS vs. meek) offers best reach vs. performance on restrictive DPI with minimal client complexity?
- Can we reliably hide TUN device presence and SOCKS5 listeners on Windows/Linux/Android without root/admin or kernel modules?
- How will clients discover/rotate fronting endpoints without leaking metadata (bootstrap via DoH/DoT vs. baked-in lists)?
- What kill-switch enforcement model is robust across platforms (network namespaces, firewall rules, system profiles) and fails closed by default?
- What telemetry schema and sampling rate balances WAU-ZB measurement with privacy and deniability (hashed fingerprints, differential privacy, optional opt-in)?
