## v0.1 scope
v0.1 ships in 2 weeks: Android client (minSdk 26) with VPNService-based tunnel, local SOCKS5 proxy hidden via unprivileged port randomization and TUN naming obfuscation, two pluggable transports (shadowsocks 2022-ietf and obfs4-like HTTP-obfs), local Room policy store, encrypted config pull via Cloudflare Workers (free tier), basic health probes and failover across 3 CDN-fronted endpoints, and minimal encrypted telemetry to Supabase Postgres (free tier) for WAUS/U calculation without PII.

## Open questions
- Which obfuscation transports (shadowsocks 2022, obfs4, or Hysteria2) provide best undetectability vs battery/throughput on stock Android without root?
- Can we reliably hide TUN interface names and SOCKS5 proxy ports from local apps under active inspection without root or system signatures?
- How will we measure WAUS/U and detection events while preserving user privacy (local differential privacy vs encrypted aggregate telemetry)?
- What is the fallback path when common CDN ports (443/80) are throttled or blocked by DPI in target jurisdictions?
- Which minimal privilege model (VPNService-only vs split tun+proxy) balances stealth, battery, and Play Policy compliance?
