## Deployment
cf workers

## v0.1 scope
v0.1 in 2 weeks: a cross-platform CLI daemon (Linux/macOS/Windows) that exposes a local SOCKS5 listener, obfuscates traffic via a simple TLS-forwarding proxy to a CF Worker endpoint (mimicking normal HTTPS), includes a best-effort local killswitch (block non-tunneled IPv4/IPv6 when enabled), randomized ephemeral ports per session, and minimal client-side telemetry (session start/end, blocked boolean, bytes) emitted to an anonymous CF KV endpoint; no TUN in v0.1, no UI, no auth, no server-side logs.

## Open questions
- Which obfuscation primitives are safest under Russian DPI (TLS-in-TLS, meek, shadowsocks 2022 AEAD, or domain-fronting) while remaining OSS and free-tier deployable?
- Can CF Workers sustain long-lived WebSocket or HTTP/2 streams for relay without breaching free-tier limits, and what is the realistic throughput ceiling?
- How will we implement a reliable client-side killswitch across Windows/macOS/Linux that blocks non-stealth traffic without requiring admin/root at every run?
- What telemetry schema and consent flow balances WAU-ZB measurement with privacy (no PII, no precise location) and avoids creating a user-identifying dataset?
- Do we need a fallback bridge (e.g., Tor bridge pluggable transports) if CF Workers endpoints are blocked, and how will clients discover/rotate them?
