## v0.1 scope
v0.1 in 2 weeks: Android app (no root) with in-process boringtun tunnel, localhost SOCKS5 hidden via iptables/nftables rules, CF Workers edge camouflage (443 DoQ front), Supabase user/auth + ephemeral session state, basic WAUS/U counter, and kill-switch that blocks cleartext when tunnel is down; manual config distribution (QR invite codes) and no usage limits on free tier.

## Open questions
- Which port-fronting strategy minimizes collateral blocking risk while maximizing reach (443 DoQ vs 443 DoH CONNECT vs domain-fronted CF Workers)?
- Can we reliably hide TUN interfaces on stock Android without root while preserving throughput (tun.so/utun vs ptrace/LKM approaches)?
- What DPI signatures (packet lengths, timing, TLS fingerprints) are most cost-effective to randomize within our free-tier budget?
- How will we measure WAUS/U without creating privacy-leaking telemetry?
- Which CA/certificate pinning bypass technique balances compatibility and detectability on common apps in restrictive jurisdictions?
